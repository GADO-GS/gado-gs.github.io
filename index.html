Abstract

3D Gaussian Splatting (3DGS) has emerged as a compelling representation for real-time novel view synthesis due to its high rendering quality and efficient rasterization. However, vanilla 3DGS jointly optimizes geometry and appearance under a photometric objective, often leading to geometry-appearance ambiguity during training, where appearance compensates for inaccurate geometry by fitting view-dependent effects. This ambiguity leads to unstable optimization, texture-baked geometry, and redundant primitive growth, affecting both efficiency and compactness. To address this issue, we propose GADO-GS, a structure-first training framework that reduces this ambiguity within the standard 3DGS pipeline. The core idea is to let the scene structure stabilize before the high-frequency appearance is fully expressed, while using cross-view geometric consistency to constrain Gaussian growth. By improving training dynamics at the source instead of relying on post-hoc compression, GADO-GS learns a more coherent and less redundant Gaussian representation. Across the three benchmark datasets, GADO-GS provides on average 10.1x faster training, 7.2x higher rendering throughput, and 8.4x smaller models than vanilla 3DGS, while maintaining competitive reconstruction quality.

Method Overview

Geometry-first optimization stabilizes scene structure early, while appearance-aware rendering progressively refines color and compactness under cross-view constraints.

Step 1

Decouple the Gaussian representation. The scene is split into geometry and appearance fields so that coarse structure can stabilize before high-frequency appearance begins to dominate training.

Step 2

Constrain view rendering explicitly. Rotation, scale, and opacity are normalized or controlled explicitly, reducing geometry-appearance ambiguity and preventing redundant Gaussian growth.

Step 3

Regularize with cross-view consistency. Multi-view geometric consistency and PSA color prediction work together to produce cleaner geometry, fewer artifacts, and a more compact final 3D Gaussian representation.
